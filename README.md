## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes an international e-commerce company's business transactions using **Tableau Dashboard** and **Tableau Story**. The objective is to uncover sales patterns, customer behavior, and business opportunities that support the company's international expansion strategy.

The analysis combines descriptive and inferential statistics with interactive visualizations to generate actionable business insights.

---

## 🏢 Background

A London-based online retail company has been selling gifts and home accessories for adults and children through its website since 2007. Its customers consist of individual consumers worldwide as well as small businesses that purchase products in bulk for resale.

Although the company already operates internationally, its sales are still concentrated in the United Kingdom. Therefore, the company aims to expand its international sales more evenly over the next five years.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective (SMART)

- **Specific:** Expand international sales more evenly across countries.
- **Measurable:** Identify factors that influence international sales performance.
- **Achievable:** The company already serves customers from multiple countries.
- **Relevant:** Reduce sales dependency on the United Kingdom.
- **Time-bound:** Achieve the expansion goal within the next **5 years**.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement

The company plans to achieve more balanced international sales over the next five years. To support this objective, it needs insights into the factors influencing sales performance and customer transactions across different countries.

---

## 📈 Business Questions

- Which countries have the highest number of transactions after the United Kingdom?
- Which products are sold the most?
- Which products are canceled most frequently?
- How many transactions were canceled in April?
- On which day of the week is the average revenue the highest? *(Descriptive Statistics)*
- Is there a statistically significant difference in average revenue among **France, Germany, and Spain**? *(Inferential Statistics)*

## 💡 Key Findings

- **France, Germany, EIRE, Spain, and Switzerland** are the top potential markets for future expansion.
- There is **no statistically significant difference** in average revenue among **France, Germany, and Spain**, indicating similar market performance.
- Best-selling products should be prioritized for international expansion.
- Frequently canceled products suggest inventory management issues, particularly stock availability.
- **Sunday** generates the highest average revenue, making it the most suitable day for product launches and promotional campaigns.
- Transaction cancellations peak in **October**, indicating the need for better inventory planning and marketing strategies during that period.

---

## ✅ Conclusion

The analysis indicates that the company has strong growth opportunities beyond the United Kingdom. Prioritizing high-potential countries, improving inventory management, and utilizing customer purchasing trends can help achieve a more balanced international sales distribution over the next five years.
